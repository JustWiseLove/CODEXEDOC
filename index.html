Can You Break Free From Lies?

To accept Bible truth, your personality matters. Some resist because they’ve been indoctrinated with lies for years. Jehovah seeks those with the right traits to break free and learn. What are they?

  1. Are you hungry for truth? A deep desire to know Jehovah drives you to seek His Word over human traditions.
  2. Are you honest with yourself? Facing the truth means admitting you might have been misled—hard, but vital for growth.
  3. Are you humble enough to change? Pride blocks learning; humility opens your heart to Jehovah’s correction.
  4. Are you open-minded to new ideas? Overcoming indoctrination requires examining the Bible without prejudice.
  5. Do you have courage to stand apart? Truth often means rejecting popular lies—courage keeps you firm.

“Happy are those hungering and thirsting for righteousness, since they will be filled.” — Matthew 5:6

“The one speaking truth declares what is right, but a false witness speaks deceit.” — Proverbs 12:17

“God opposes the haughty ones, but he gives undeserved kindness to the humble ones.” — James 4:6

“They accepted the word with the greatest eagerness of mind, carefully examining the Scriptures daily.” — Acts 17:11

“Be courageous and strong… for Jehovah your God is with you.” — Joshua 1:9

These traits—hunger, honesty, humility, open-mindedness, and courage—equip you to see past a lifetime of lies and embrace Jehovah’s truth!

Are You Ready For The Truth?

Embracing Bible truth requires preparation. Jehovah offers it to those willing to align their lives with His standards. Are you ready to take the steps?

  1. Do you seek understanding? A desire to know Jehovah’s will opens your mind to His teachings.
  2. Are you willing to adjust? True readiness means changing habits to match Bible principles.
  3. Do you trust Jehovah’s guidance? Faith in His direction leads you to the truth.
  4. Are you persistent in learning? Consistent study builds a strong foundation in truth.
  5. Do you share with others? Spreading truth shows your readiness to live it.

“Keep on seeking, and you will find.” — Matthew 7:7

“Remove the wicked one from before the king, and his throne will be firmly established in righteousness.” — Proverbs 25:5

“Trust in Jehovah with all your heart.” — Proverbs 3:5

“Become doers of the word, and not hearers only.” — James 1:22

“Go therefore and make disciples… teaching them.” — Matthew 28:19-20

Readiness—seeking, adjusting, trusting, persisting, and sharing—prepares you to fully accept and live Jehovah’s truth!
