Can You Break Free From Lies?

To accept Bible truth, your personality matters. Some resist because they’ve been indoctrinated with lies for years. Jehovah seeks those with the right traits to break free and learn. What are they?

  1. Are you hungry for truth? A deep desire to know Jehovah drives you to seek His Word over human traditions.

    2. Happy are those hungering and thirsting for righteousness, since they will be filled. — Matthew 5:6

  2. Are you honest with yourself? Facing the truth means admitting you might have been misled—hard, but vital for growth.

    3. The one speaking truth declares what is right, but a false witness speaks deceit. — Proverbs 12:17

  3. Are you humble enough to change? Pride blocks learning; humility opens your heart to Jehovah’s correction.

    4. God opposes the haughty ones, but he gives undeserved kindness to the humble ones. — James 4:6

  4. Are you open-minded to new ideas? Overcoming indoctrination requires examining the Bible without prejudice.

    5. They accepted the word with the greatest eagerness of mind, carefully examining the Scriptures daily. — Acts 17:11

  5. Do you have courage to stand apart? Truth often means rejecting popular lies—courage keeps you firm.

    6. Be courageous and strong… for Jehovah your God is with you. — Joshua 1:9

These traits—hunger, honesty, humility, open-mindedness, and courage—equip you to see past a lifetime of lies and embrace Jehovah’s truth!

Are You Ready For The Truth?

Truth from Jehovah’s Word is a precious gift, but accepting it requires a heart prepared to act. Are you ready to let the Bible guide your life? Consider these qualities that show readiness for truth.

  1. Do you seek Jehovah with all your heart? A sincere search for God leads to His truth, found in the Scriptures.

    2. You will seek me and find me, because you will search for me with all your heart. — Jeremiah 29:13

  2. Are you willing to obey God’s standards? Truth demands action—obedience to Jehovah’s commands brings you closer to Him.

    3. The one who does the will of my Father who is in heaven is the one who will enter the Kingdom. — Matthew 7:21

  3. Do you value wisdom over pride? Accepting truth means prioritizing Jehovah’s wisdom over personal opinions.

    4. The fear of Jehovah is the beginning of wisdom. — Proverbs 9:10

  4. Are you patient in learning? Understanding Jehovah’s purpose takes time and diligent study of His Word.

    5. Take your time and be patient, so that you may understand the words. — 2 Timothy 2:7

  5. Do you trust Jehovah’s promises? Faith in His Word strengthens you to embrace truth, even when it challenges you.

    6. God is not a man that he should lie… Has he said it and will he not do it? — Numbers 23:19

With a heart that seeks, obeys, values wisdom, remains patient, and trusts Jehovah, you are ready to embrace the truth of His Word and live by it!
