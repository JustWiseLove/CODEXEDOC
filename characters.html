CHARACTERS

Jesus Christ

Jesus Christ, the firstborn Son of Jehovah, stands as the ultimate example of perfect love and obedience, offering his life as a ransom to redeem humankind from sin and death. Born in Bethlehem to the virgin Mary, he grew up in Nazareth, working as a carpenter until he began his ministry at age 30. For three and a half years, Jesus traveled through Judea and Galilee, teaching profound truths about Jehovah’s Kingdom, healing the sick, and raising the dead, all while displaying humility and compassion. Despite facing betrayal by Judas Iscariot and a brutal execution on a torture stake, Jesus remained faithful, fulfilling prophecies like Isaiah 53:5 about being “pierced for our transgression.” His resurrection on the third day proved Jehovah’s approval and secured hope for everlasting life for all who follow him. Jesus’ life teaches us to prioritize Jehovah’s will, love our enemies, and trust in God’s promises, no matter the cost.

For I have given you an example, that you should do just as I have done to you. — John 13:15

This is my Son, the beloved, whom I have approved; listen to him. — Matthew 17:5

Abraham

Abraham, originally named Abram, emerged as a towering figure of faith, leaving his comfortable life in Ur of the Chaldeans at Jehovah’s command to journey to a land he did not know. At 75 years old, he packed up his household, including his wife Sarah and nephew Lot, and traveled to Canaan, trusting Jehovah’s promise to make him a great nation despite having no children at the time. His faith was tested repeatedly—waiting decades for the birth of his son Isaac, separating from Lot, and facing the ultimate trial when Jehovah asked him to offer Isaac as a sacrifice, a request Abraham obeyed until Jehovah provided a ram instead. Abraham’s unwavering trust earned him the title “Jehovah’s friend,” and his obedience foreshadowed the faith required for salvation through Christ. His life shows us the power of trusting Jehovah completely, even when His promises seem impossible.

By faith Abraham, when he was called, obeyed by going out to a place he was to receive as an inheritance; he went out, although not knowing where he was going. — Hebrews 11:8

By faith Abraham, when he was tested, as good as offered up Isaac—the man who had gladly received the promises attempted to offer up his only-begotten son. — Hebrews 11:17

David

David, the youngest son of Jesse from Bethlehem, rose from a humble shepherd to become Israel’s greatest king, known as a man after Jehovah’s own heart because of his deep love for God and genuine repentance. As a youth, he fearlessly faced the giant Goliath with a sling and stone, trusting Jehovah to deliver him, an act of courage that inspired Israel. David’s life was marked by both triumphs—like uniting the nation and bringing the ark of the covenant to Jerusalem—and failures, such as his sin with Bath-sheba and the census that displeased Jehovah. Yet, each time he stumbled, David turned back to Jehovah with heartfelt repentance, as seen in his psalms of sorrow and praise. His poetic writings, like Psalm 23, reveal a deep trust in Jehovah as his shepherd, guiding him through life’s trials. David’s example teaches us to rely on Jehovah’s strength, seek forgiveness sincerely, and worship God with all our heart.

Jehovah is my shepherd; I will lack nothing. — Psalm 23:1

After removing Saul, he made David their king. He also bore witness about him and said, ‘I have found David the son of Jesse a man agreeable to my heart; he will carry out all my will.’ — Acts 13:22
